hi, i'm johnny nguyen, a first-generation cs student from fort wayne, indiana, studying computer science on the systems programming track at purdue. i write a lot of c and c++, and i'm most interested in systems programming. i have built things in c and c++ like a unix shell, a malloc implementation, and an http server.

this summer i'm interning at sandia national laboratories in albuquerque, nm, working on dataflow architecture simulation by modeling how general-purpose programs execute on spatial accelerators and exploring instruction-level optimizations using the structural simulation toolkit (sst). this is really exciting to me since i have enjoyed taking cs250 and cs252 at purdue and i want to apply those classes toward an actual project.

i also do undergraduate research with rtx through purdue's the data mine, simulating rf signals for downstream classification models, and occasionally i build things for fun. for example, i have made a bad apple!! renderer in spectrograms.
